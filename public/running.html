Why I run

Even though running may seem boring just by using only your 2 legs to move, I feel that it adds a lot of positives to it.

It helps me to manage my stress levels and it sets myself new challenges to tackle with, such as finishing 15 km of running in a single day.

How I came back to running:

Before coming back to running, I didn't have much of a positive mood as compared to now. Exercise wasn't much of a thing to me, and I wasn't conscious of its benefits.

Even though I was in a fitness club when I was a kid, I haven't developed the interest in running yet. However, it gave me a number of opportunities for me to exercise.

Thanks to the workout options on my Apple Watch (and Strava), it helps me manage and track my workouts, especially for running. It also gets me more motivated to exercise!

My Memorable runs


Queenstown NZ Long Run

I felt that it's a good challenge, as this was my first successful attempt of a half marathon distance. I liked the cooling weather and the scenery as well!


Chinese Garden (Jurong) to Botanic Gardens

During my last few km it was pretty tiring, but overall I felt that I've achieved a great sense of accomplishment after finishing it! To me, I find running in new places fascinating as I get to try routes that I haven't explored before!


18km Birthday Run for my 18th birthday

I wanted to do something special during the day after my birthday - so I decided to do 1 km for 1 year I've lived.

Again, another exhausting run during the later portions where I was literally 'steaming' when I stopped at the 12.5km mark! Because of that, I walked for a while when I was in need for air.

But I tried my best and finished the 18 km!

Overall, another great experience!
